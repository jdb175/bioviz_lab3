Game of life

About

This is a simulation of Conway's game of life. The grid of cells can either be alive (blue) or dead (gray). The behavior of these cells is based on four rules:

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if caused by under-population.
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overcrowding.
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

Controls

  • Click or drag on the grid to paint live cells on the board.
  • Hit space to start or stop the simulation. It will stop automatically if no changes are occurring.
  • Use left and right arrows to go one generation forward or back on the timeline. This will not actually simulate as long as there is available history, only replay saved states.
  • The graph along the bottom shows population over generations.
  • Click the bottom to jump to generation. Up to 400 previous generations will be saved. Clicking forward on the bottom will fastfoward to that generation.
  • Use the bottom buttons to reset the board to empty or randomize it.
